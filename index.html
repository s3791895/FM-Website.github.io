Interest in IT

My interest in IT started when I was quite young, I used video editing software on both Macintosh and Windows systems to create special effects and short films, inspired by YouTube channels such as FreddieW. Later on, I became interested in gaming through the gateway drug known as Minecraft. As I became more interested in gaming and video-editing, I sought out to build a PC that could run Borderlands 2 at higher than the 20 fps my old Mac provided. This led me to research PC parts and learn more about how computer systems work. I had to learn to effectively troubleshoot out of necessity due to receiving faulty graphics cards and powers supplies as well as a wealth of internet problems. I also learned how to make basic modifications to programs such as editing ‘.ini’ files in programs and games (specifically Bethesda games). While it was annoying to have to fix these problems and/or bad design decisions it motivated me to learn and apply skills to improve the functionality and usability of products and services within the realm of IT.

I chose to go to RMIT as it has a prestigious reputation in the field of information technology and as a bonus is located relatively close to me and convenient to get to. The Bachelor of Information Technology provides many specialisations from which I could choose. A focus on hands-on studying also appeals to me rather than bogging everything down in theory with no end products. There is also a lot of things to do in the city so there will be something to do before, after and in between classes.

I expect the Bachelor of Information Technology to provide a broad range of courses, allowing me to choose what to learn from a range of specialisations, giving me flexibility within the IT sector. Specific things I expect to learn include an understanding of the java programming language and how to utilise it to fulfil tasks for certain needs. As well as how to effectively communicate IT ideas and designs to developers as well as users.

Ideal Job

UX/UI Designer - Bupa ANZ (link here)

Working on improving the user experience for customers throughout Bupa ANZ’s websites and digital services. Will involve multiple projects allowing for variety rather than working on the same thing over and over. Will use research to assist in developing possible prototypes and testing their usability and engagement using UCD heuristics. Involves communicating with customers and using feedback and research results to balance their needs with those of the business. This is appealing as there is a decent amount of variety involved (research, communication and development). Also, more creative than the majority of IT jobs due to the focus on design and provides an end product I believe to be very important due to experience with poor UX design.

The position requires lots of experience using design apps (Photoshop, Illustrator, Azure and others among those listed). You must also be able to validate design templates and prototypes through a range of methodologies and be well versed in the development of CX artefacts such as personas, storyboards and customer journey maps. Adaptability is also important as users’ feedback could be completely different to what was expected, and you must be able to quickly pivot to attend to their needs.

I am relatively experienced with photoshop and similar apps, however, I have little to no experience with apps such as Illustrator or Azure. I have some experience in modifying user interfaces for myself, for example modifying the PC games with HUDs I found frustrating and/or intrusive such as ‘Fallout 4’ and ‘Dark Souls’ as well as editing the GUI for other programs such as Linux MultiMedia Studio (LMMS). Though these changes were only made to suit me personally rather than a large userbase so I would need to develop my surveying and researching skills.

To obtain and develop the required skills and experience I will continue to use image editing software such as photoshop and begin to learn how best to utilise Illustrator and Azure through tutorials and experimentation. To help in identifying what makes a good user experience I will take note of why a website or program is annoying or nice to use rather than just saying it is good or bad. Development of surveying and research skills will be facilitated by RMIT’s UCD course as the current assignment focuses on just that and future assignments will also prepare me better for the role.
